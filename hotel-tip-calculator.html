Hotel Tipping Guide

Tipping hotel staff is customary in many countries, particularly in the United States and Canada. Understanding when and how much to tip can help ensure good service while avoiding awkwardness or overspending.

General Hotel Tipping Guidelines

Staff Position Typical Tip Amount
Housekeeping $2-5 per night
Bellhop/Porter $1-2 per bag (min. $5)
Concierge $5-20 depending on service
Room Service 15-20% of bill (if not included)
Valet $2-5 each time car is retrieved
Doorman $1-2 for hailing cab, $1-4 for luggage help

Hotel Tipping Etiquette

Housekeeping Tips

Leave housekeeping tips daily rather than at the end of your stay, as different staff may clean your room each day. Place the tip on the pillow or desk with a note saying "Thank you" to ensure it's recognized as a tip.

Currency and Timing

Always tip in local currency when possible. For services like bellhop assistance or valet, have small bills ready in advance to avoid awkwardly searching for money when the service is completed.

International Considerations

Tipping practices vary widely by country. In some countries like Japan, tipping may even be considered offensive. Research local customs before traveling internationally.

Included Gratuities

Some hotels add service charges or gratuities automatically, particularly for room service. Check your bill carefully to avoid double-tipping. If a service charge is included but the service was exceptional, you can still add a small additional tip.
