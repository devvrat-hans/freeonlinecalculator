Dog Groomer Tipping Guide

Tipping your dog groomer is a common practice to show appreciation for their skill and care. Dog grooming is physically demanding work that requires training, patience, and attention to detail. Standard tipping rates typically range from 15-20% of the service cost, but may vary based on several factors.

Suggested Tip Percentages by Service

  • Basic Bath & Brush: 15%
  • Full Grooming: 15-20%
  • Specialty Grooming: 20-25%
  • Nail Trim Only: $2-5 flat tip
  • De-shedding Treatment: 15-20%

When to Tip More for Dog Grooming

Consider tipping more generously when:

  • Your dog is challenging: If your pet is anxious, uncooperative, or requires special handling
  • Your dog's coat was matted: Matted fur requires extra time and careful attention
  • It's a large dog: Larger dogs require more physical effort to handle and groom
  • Special styling was done: Detailed cuts, hand scissoring, or special requests
  • Holiday season: During busy holiday periods when groomers are working extra hours
  • Last-minute appointment: If the groomer accommodated a last-minute request
  • Regular relationship: If you see the same groomer regularly, holiday or seasonal bonuses are appreciated

Dog Grooming Tipping Etiquette

Here are some tipping etiquette guidelines for pet grooming services:

  • Tip the Groomer Directly: If possible, give the tip directly to the person who groomed your dog
  • Cash is Preferred: While cards are often accepted, cash tips are generally preferred
  • Salon Owner: If the salon owner grooms your dog, tipping is still appreciated but not always expected
  • Mobile Groomers: Mobile groomers typically receive the same tip percentages as salon-based groomers
  • Multiple Pets: Consider tipping 15-20% on the total bill or a slightly lower percentage with a minimum of $5 per pet
  • Holiday Bonus: For regular clients, an extra-generous tip during the holidays (equal to one service) is a nice gesture
