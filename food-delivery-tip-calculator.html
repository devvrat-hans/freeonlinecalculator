Food Delivery Tipping Guide

When tipping food delivery drivers, consider these factors:

  • Standard Tipping: 15-20% of the subtotal (before tax, fees, and discounts)
  • Minimum Tips: Even for small orders, a minimum of $4-5 is recommended
  • Distance: Tip more if the driver traveled far to reach you
  • Complexity: Large or complex orders deserve higher tips
  • Weather: Add extra for deliveries during rain, snow, or extreme temperatures
  • Difficulty: Consider adding more for difficult delivery locations
  • Time of Day: Late night, weekend, or holiday deliveries warrant higher tips

App-Based vs. Direct Restaurant Delivery Tipping

Tipping expectations may vary based on the delivery service:

  • Direct Restaurant Delivery: Typically 15-20%, as drivers often receive lower hourly wages
  • DoorDash/UberEats/Grubhub: 15-20% or minimum $3-5
  • Service Fees: Remember that delivery fees usually go to the company, not the driver
  • Tipping in App: Most apps allow tipping before or after delivery
  • Cash Tips: Some drivers prefer cash tips as they receive them immediately

Remember that many food delivery drivers rely heavily on tips as a significant portion of their income.

Why Food Delivery Tips Matter

Food delivery drivers often:

  • Use their own vehicles and pay for gas out of pocket
  • Receive minimal base pay from delivery apps, making tips essential
  • Deal with traffic, parking challenges, and navigation
  • Ensure your food arrives hot and in good condition
  • Work during peak meal times, weekends, and bad weather when you'd rather stay home
  • Handle multiple orders simultaneously to maximize efficiency

A fair tip acknowledges their service and helps ensure continued good service for future orders.
