Grocery Delivery Tipping Guide

When tipping grocery delivery workers, consider these factors:

  • Standard Tipping: 10-20% of the total bill is customary
  • Minimum Tips: Even for small orders, a minimum of $3-5 is recommended
  • Item Count: More items mean more time spent shopping and organizing
  • Heavy Items: Consider extra tip for orders with cases of water, pet food, etc.
  • Distance: Tip more if the driver traveled far to reach you
  • Weather: Add extra for deliveries during rain, snow, or extreme temperatures
  • Difficulty: Consider adding more for walk-up apartments or hard-to-access locations

Why Tip Grocery Delivery Workers?

Grocery delivery workers:

  • Shop for all your items, making sure to select quality produce and check expiration dates
  • Navigate through crowded stores, especially during peak shopping hours
  • Make substitution decisions when items are out of stock
  • Often use their personal vehicles and pay for gas
  • May carry heavy items to your door, climbing stairs when needed
  • Work during all weather conditions to ensure you get essential groceries

A fair tip acknowledges their effort and helps ensure quality service for future orders.

Grocery Delivery App Tipping

Different grocery delivery platforms may have different tipping guidelines:

  • Instacart: Typically recommends 5% as a default but 15-20% is more appropriate
  • Amazon Fresh: Has a tipping option during checkout, with 15% being standard
  • Shipt: Recommends 15% of the order or a minimum of $5
  • Walmart Delivery: Allows tipping after delivery, with 10-15% being common
  • Local Grocery Services: Usually suggest 10-20% based on service

Remember that many delivery workers rely heavily on tips as a significant portion of their income.
