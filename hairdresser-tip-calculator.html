Hairdresser Tipping Guide

Tipping your hairdresser or stylist is a common practice that shows appreciation for their service and skill. The appropriate tip amount can vary based on several factors including service complexity, quality, and relationship with your stylist.

Standard Tipping Guidelines

  • 15-20% is the standard range for most hair services
  • 20-25% for exceptional service or complex techniques
  • $3-10 for assistants who shampoo or help with services
  • 15-30% during holidays or for special appointments

Tipping by Service Type

  • Haircut: 15-20% of service cost
  • Coloring: 18-22% (more for complex color work)
  • Highlights/Balayage: 20-25% (these are time-intensive services)
  • Blowout/Styling: 15-20% of service cost
  • Treatments: 15-20% of service cost

Salon Tipping Etiquette

Who to Tip

In a salon setting, multiple people may assist with your service. Here's who typically receives tips:

  • Main Stylist/Colorist: The primary professional who performs your cut or color
  • Shampoo Person/Assistant: Staff who wash your hair or assist the stylist
  • Additional Service Providers: If you receive services from multiple staff members

Tipping FAQ

Should I tip the salon owner?

Traditionally, salon owners were excluded from tipping, but this norm has changed. If the owner performs your service personally, tipping the same amount as you would any stylist is appropriate.

How do I handle holiday tipping?

During holiday seasons, consider tipping more generously (up to 30% or the cost of one service) as a thank you for year-round service, especially if you're a regular client.

What if I'm unhappy with the service?

If you're dissatisfied, first communicate your concerns to the stylist or manager to see if they can rectify the issue. If moderate adjustments are made, a reduced tip of 10% may be appropriate. For significant problems, discuss with management before deciding on the tip.
